Degree Does Not Equal Ability

Investigating South Africa's IT Skills Gap

A workforce analytics and skills demand assessment comparing graduate output, digital vacancies, employment indicators, and skills mismatch evidence.

Project Overview

This project examines the information gap between education output and employer demand in South Africa's technology labour market.

The analysis consolidates public education, employment, vacancy, and skills demand indicators into an evidence framework that supports workforce planning discussions.

The business outcome is a clearer view of where qualifications, practical evidence, entry-level pathways, and skills demand need to be assessed together.

Business Challenge

Employers continue reporting digital skills shortages while graduates enter the labour market with formal qualifications. The difficult question is not whether degrees matter, but whether qualifications alone provide enough evidence of workplace readiness.

Without a consolidated reporting view, education output, vacancy pressure, graduate outcomes, and skills mismatch indicators are difficult to compare.

Data Sources

The evidence base uses public statistics and research indicators covering university graduate output, ICT employment, unfilled digital roles, junior role demand, and skills mismatch rates.

Each source was grouped by theme, metric, source confidence, and analyst interpretation to keep the conclusions traceable.

Data Processing and Analysis

I cleaned numeric records, separated weak rows for review, grouped evidence by category and source, and created KPI indicators for graduate supply, demand pressure, entry-level barriers, and mismatch evidence.

The analysis focuses on workforce trends, labour market pressure, education-to-employment alignment, and evidence based reporting.

Tools and Technologies

Excel, data analysis, labor market analytics, research analysis, data storytelling, business intelligence.

Reporting and Insights

  • 220,758 public HEI university graduates were identified in the evidence base.
  • 118,000 total unfilled digital roles were recorded as a demand pressure indicator.
  • 41,000 junior level digital roles remain unfilled, showing an entry level pathway challenge.
  • The skills mismatch rate was estimated at 50 percent, signalling misalignment between available skills and required skills.

Business Outcome

This project reframes the hiring conversation from degree versus skill to degree plus evidence of ability. It helps graduates understand why portfolios, real projects, certifications, and tool fluency matter, while helping employers and educators see where junior pathways need stronger practical alignment.

Key Analytical Capabilities

The project demonstrates evidence based analysis, KPI development, data quality judgment, labor market interpretation, dashboard communication, and executive storytelling.
