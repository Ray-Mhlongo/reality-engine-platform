SQL Server and Excel business intelligence

PC Parts Market Intelligence

Market research and analytics for the South African computer hardware industry.

This project combines SQL, Excel, and business intelligence techniques to analyze pricing, manufacturers, product categories, regional availability, inventory risk, and retailer trends.

Project Overview

Business problem: PC part prices vary across retailers, provinces, brands, categories, and stock levels. Buyers and business teams need a structured way to understand fair pricing, market performance, inventory pressure, and retailer behavior.

Objective: Build a complete market intelligence solution that tracks product pricing, sales activity, inventory levels, retailer performance, customer purchasing behavior, and historical price movement.

Methodology: I designed a relational SQL Server database, created realistic market records, wrote analytical SQL queries, and used Excel to build dashboards for executive reporting and business insight.

Outcome: The final workbook and SQL model reveal category revenue strength, pricing variation, inventory risk, customer behavior, and retailer performance in one connected reporting workflow.

Technologies Used

SQL Server Management Studio, T SQL, Excel, pivot style analysis, dashboard design, data modeling, business intelligence, data visualization.

Key Findings

  • GPU and monitor categories generated the strongest revenue.
  • Pricing varied noticeably between retailers and provinces.
  • Inventory risk appeared in low stock and out of stock listings.
  • Customer segment analysis helped reveal different purchasing behaviors.
  • Retailer benchmarking made it easier to compare revenue, stock, and pricing performance.

Business Impact

The project helps decision makers compare prices, monitor stock risk, benchmark retailers, understand customer demand, and identify where pricing or inventory strategy needs attention. It turns scattered product and transaction records into a usable market intelligence system.

What This Project Demonstrates

This project demonstrates relational database design, SQL joins and aggregations, window functions, data modeling, Excel dashboard reporting, pricing analysis, inventory analysis, customer analysis, and business storytelling.
