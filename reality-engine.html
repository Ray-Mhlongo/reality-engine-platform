Why it is different

Reality Engine is not a dashboard.

Many reporting tools show what happened only after the user knows which metrics to inspect. Reality Engine is designed to support earlier analytical triage.

Upload a CSV or Excel file and the workflow reviews data quality, identifies trends, highlights anomalies, surfaces risks and opportunities, forecasts possible outcomes, and organizes findings into decision-ready outputs.

The purpose is not to replace analysis. It provides a repeatable first-pass reporting framework that helps business users move from raw files to clearer questions and actions.

Case study

Building a decision intelligence and reporting workflow

Problem

Dashboards often wait for users to know the right question. Many teams need an initial analytical review that highlights data quality, trends, risks, and decision points.

Solution

Reality Engine analyzes uploaded datasets, profiles quality, identifies trends and anomalies, supports scenario testing, and produces reporting outputs.

Capabilities

Data profiling, relationship maps, forecasting, scenario analysis, opportunity scanning, warnings, guided questioning, and executive reporting.

Tech Stack

React, Tailwind CSS, Chart.js, JavaScript, CSV/XLSX parsing, local processing, Apps Script path, PostgreSQL path, OpenRouter path.

Business Outcome

Reality Engine reduces the manual effort required to move from raw data inspection to risk review, opportunity identification, and decision support.

What I Learned

I learned to design analytics around decisions, not charts, and to connect data quality, reporting logic, and business analysis into one workflow.

Future Roadmap

Authentication, persistent workspaces, external data connectors, stronger data lineage, deeper forecasting controls, and role-based reporting templates.
